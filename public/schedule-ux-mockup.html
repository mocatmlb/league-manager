Schedule UX — Decision 1

How should the schedule default? Pick the option that feels right.

Decision 1 of 1
Default view for the public schedule
The schedule has past results and upcoming games. These three options each answer "where do you land when you open the page?" differently. Today = June 6, 2026 in these mockups.
● Option A
Show All — Scroll to Today
Full schedule loads. Past games are visually muted. Page auto-scrolls to the first game today or upcoming. "Today's Games" button in the header jumps there manually.
History always visible — no extra click
Shareable links always show full schedule
First load scrolls — can feel like a flash
Long seasons = lots of muted rows above the fold

● Option B
Default to Upcoming Only
Page loads showing only games from today forward. A banner at the top lets you opt into full history. This is how most sports apps (ESPN, MLB) handle it.
Zero scrolling — land right on current games
Clean, fast, immediately useful
Past results require an extra click
URL without param hides history — could surprise users

● Option C
Tabs: Upcoming & Results
Two explicit tabs. "Upcoming" shows today + future. "Results" shows completed past games. Counts in the tabs give a quick summary at a glance.
Clearest mental model — two intents, two views
Results tab can be sorted differently (most recent first)
More UI chrome — tabs take up space
Game in progress today appears in both tabs
